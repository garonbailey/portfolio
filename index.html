Bio

I started building websites as a hobby when I was about 14-years-old, but I went to Auburn University with the plan to become a veterinarian, majoring in biology. Life seldom works out how you expect, though. I would switch my major to Interdisciplinary Studies, concentrating on English and writing before taking time off a few credit hours short of graduating. That's when I went to New York City to attend General Assembly's Web Development Immersive bootcamp course to change my career. Ever since, I've been working as a developer with a plan to eventually go back to finish that degree that I spent quite a bit of time and energy on.


In my spare time, I play in an ice hockey league. I watch a lot of movies. Occasionally, I try to work on film projects as well, particularly enjoying writing stories or doing video work. I have played with cameras since I was about 12-years-old, even working as an event photographer and student newspaper photo editor for a time during college. I particularly enjoy following the Nashville Predators hockey team and Gritty, the enigmatic mascot of the Philadelphia Flyers. I'll play tennis if I feel like getting mad at myself.


I'm happy to collaborate on a variety of projects, whether they're web-based, writing, or involve my camera.
